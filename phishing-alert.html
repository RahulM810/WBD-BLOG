Security Alert: New Phishing Tactics Targeting Banks

Issued: January 28, 2024 | Threat Level: High

What We're Seeing

Security researchers have identified a sophisticated phishing campaign targeting customers of major banks with fraudulent emails that appear to come from legitimate financial institutions.

Key Characteristics

  • Emails with subject lines like "Important Account Notification" or "Security Alert"
  • Logos and branding that closely mimic legitimate banks
  • Links to fake login pages that capture credentials
  • Urgent language requiring immediate action

Example of a fraudulent email currently circulating

How to Protect Yourself

Do:
  • Verify sender email addresses carefully
  • Hover over links before clicking
  • Contact your bank directly if unsure
  • Use official banking apps when possible
Don't:
  • Click links in unexpected emails
  • Enter credentials after following email links
  • Provide personal info in response to emails
  • Open attachments from unknown senders

Report Phishing

If you receive a suspicious email:

  1. Don't click any links
  2. Forward to your bank's fraud department
  3. Report to FTC
  4. Delete the email
